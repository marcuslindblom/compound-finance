## Why — Signal and Supporting Evidence

**Primary signal:** Two board members — Tomas Bergström and Staffan Salén — each bought exactly 263,978 shares at 31.08 SEK on 2026-04-30, for a combined total of 16.4 MSEK. The transaction is notable for its precision: identical share counts, identical price, same date, executed while the stock sits at its 52-week low after a catastrophic two-week drop from 44 SEK.

**Supporting evidence:**

1. **Capital at risk is substantial.** 8.2 MSEK per person is not a routine portfolio adjustment. For board members — who typically have lower compensation exposure than executives — this level of capital commitment suggests genuine conviction in the stock's recovery potential.

2. **Size relative to market cap is exceptional.** Combined 16.4 MSEK into a ~1.25 BSEK company equals approximately 1.3% of estimated market cap purchased in a single day. This is an unusually large proportional signal.

3. **Contra-trend behavior.** The stock dropped ~30% in roughly two weeks (from ~44 SEK to ~30 SEK), with a particularly violent single-day drop on April 29 (43.70 to 32.30, -26%). Buying at the exact point of maximum pain rather than waiting for stabilization adds information content to the signal — insiders appear to view the selloff as mispricing rather than fundamental deterioration.

4. **Stock at 52-week low.** At 30.60 SEK, the stock is at the lowest price in a year. The 52-week high was 69.50 SEK — the stock has lost 56% of its peak value. Board members are buying at the lowest price available, not chasing a partial recovery.

5. **Coordination signal.** Two independent individuals buying the exact same number of shares on the same day at the same price is not coincidental. This implies either a coordinated board-level view that the crash represents a buying opportunity, or a pre-agreed structured transaction (e.g. a block purchase arranged simultaneously). Either interpretation suggests board-level alignment on valuation.

## Review

**Verdict: CAUTION** — Proceed with halved position size (1% = 10,000 SEK).

**Bull case assessment:** The 16.4 MSEK combined insider buying (~1.3% of market cap) is genuinely unusual — 13x above the Lakonishok & Lee academic threshold for predictive signals. If the crash was panic-driven (forced seller, fund liquidation), board members with knowledge of actual business conditions have a real information edge.

**Strongest bear case:** The identical block (263,978 shares each, same price, same day) is most likely a pre-arranged block absorption — not independent market conviction. A major shareholder may have needed to exit (margin call, fund redemption) and the board arranged to absorb the block to prevent a public secondary offering. If so, the signal quality collapses: insiders took a pre-structured allocation, not a spontaneous valuation bet.

**Critical red flags:**
1. **Crash cause completely unknown.** A -26% single-day move on a small-cap almost always has a specific trigger (profit warning, covenant breach, accounting issue). Entering before identifying this is paying for binary risk you cannot price.
2. **Debt-exposed services business.** Green Landscaping has grown via acquisitions = structural leverage risk. A -26% crash is consistent with covenant trigger or refinancing stress.
3. **No CEO/CFO participation.** If the crash were truly a buying opportunity, why aren't operational executives buying? Their absence is information.
4. **Stock continued falling after insider buy.** Insiders bought at 31.08, stock closed at 30.60 — market is not treating the buy as a floor signal.

**Biases identified:** Anchoring (42 SEK target based on pre-crash level, not fundamentals), authority bias (board ≠ omniscient), availability bias (striking narrative suppressing scrutiny).

**Position size recommendation:** Maximum 1% (10,000 SEK) with hard stop-loss 25.50 SEK. No adding until crash cause understood. The R:R structure makes financial risk manageable but information risk is the real exposure.
